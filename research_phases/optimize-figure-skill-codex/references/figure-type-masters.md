# Figure Type Masters

Use these as layout/task constraints. Domain masters decide scientific objects; figure types decide organization.

## Technical Roadmap / Research Workflow

Purpose: show research stages, inputs, method path, and outputs.

Use:
- 3-6 major steps, clear reading direction, input/output boundaries, branch or feedback arrows if needed.
- Labels for step names and key modules only.
- Avoid turning the workflow into a decorative poster or inventing extra steps.

## Model Architecture / System Diagram

Purpose: show components, data flow, model internals, or system interaction.

Use:
- Explicit modules, nested submodules, data/tensor/document flow, training/inference split when relevant.
- Orthogonal or curved arrows with semantic distinctions such as main flow, feedback, retrieval, loss, or control.
- Avoid generic boxes without scientific objects, ambiguous arrows, and random icons.

## Mechanism Explanation

Purpose: explain a reaction, regulation, transformation, disease pathway, or algorithmic mechanism.

Use:
- Core object/process, causal chain, activation/inhibition/transfer arrows, before/after or multi-scale zoom if useful.
- Few but accurate labels.
- Avoid unsupported intermediate mechanisms, invented parameters, and visual effects that replace scientific relations.

## Graphical Abstract / Main Figure

Purpose: compress the research problem, method, key process, and contribution into one memorable academic visual.

Use:
- Problem -> method -> outcome narrative or central discovery with supporting modules.
- Strong visual hierarchy, refined academic finish, controlled short labels.
- Avoid stuffing the whole paper into one figure or exaggerating conclusions.

## Multi-Panel Comparison

Purpose: compare methods, conditions, materials, stages, or groups.

Use:
- Unified panel grid, consistent scale/style, explicit group names, shared visual grammar.
- Do not invent axes, heatmaps, p-values, microscopy, or numeric results unless provided.

## Presentation Overview

Purpose: communicate a research plan or project overview in a slide-like academic figure.

Use:
- Central topic plus 2-4 research modules, or background -> method -> expected output.
- More generous spacing than paper figures, but keep scientific structure.
- Avoid business pitch-deck style and decorative filler.
