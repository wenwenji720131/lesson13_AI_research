# Phase 9 Paper Revision

Read `../README.md`, the current manuscript, source data, and all feedback. Work from a tracked revision plan.

## Workflow

1. Create `revision-register.md` with version, issue ID, source, severity, affected claim, action, evidence needed, owner, and status.
2. Audit every central claim against raw results, configurations, citations, figures, and stated limitations. Correct, qualify, or remove unsupported claims.
3. Classify feedback as factual correction, clarity, evidence gap, design concern, scope extension, or formatting/compliance. Address repeated and core-credibility issues first.
4. Make revisions traceable in `change-log.md`; rerun relevant validation after substantive text, code, or result changes.
5. Write `response-to-reviewers.md` with the comment, respectful response, exact change, evidence, and manuscript location. State constraints plainly when a request cannot be completed.
6. Complete `submission-check.md`: PDF build, references, figure/table consistency, authorship, anonymity or camera-ready state, ethics, supplements, and venue requirements.

## Boundaries

Do not improve scores by hiding negative results, adding unsupported citations, changing protocols without disclosure, or claiming a reviewer misunderstanding without clarifying the manuscript. Keep submission, review, and camera-ready versions separate.
